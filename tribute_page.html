INTRODUCTION

Nikola Tesla is a giant of electricity. He became one of the most importants engineers of the history, and that's because all his contributions, inventions and theories were essential to make the electricity (and something more) the way it is nowadays. In this brief tribute, we will board some of the most important facts of his life.

The most remembered story of Nikola Tesla is that he went into a hard legally battle against Thomas Alva Edison to establish the way that electricity should be served to people. This is known as the DC vs. AC war. Even though Tesla lost this battle on that moment, the time gave him the reason. AC power is the most used today because it has many advantages over DC power.

Apart from this, he is probably the biggest inspiration for scientist nowadays, because he always worked to create many alternatives (registering over 300 patents), and give the humanity all the solutions that he thought were necessary. By the moment of his death, he was going on a situation of extreme poverty because all the money he made was invested into new projects and new inventions. A true hero!

Here's a brief Time-Line of Tesla's life:
  • 1856: Born on July 10th in Smiljan, Austrian Empire.
  • 1875: He enrolled the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz Austria. During the 1st year he never missed a lecture and always got top qualifications. By the third year, he got an addiction to gambling and never finished school.
  • 1881: Tesla moved to Budapest, Hungary to work on the Budapest Telephone Exchange. During His employment, Tesla made many improvements to the Central Station equipment and claimed to have perfected a telephone repeater.
  • 1882: Tesla went to Paris and started working with the Continental Edison Company. He started then showing his big potential making improved versions of generating dynamos and motors.
  • 1884: Moved to USA due to his big ability doing engineering. After working 6 months on the Edison Machine Work, he decided to quit due to the treatment he got there.
  • 1887: After 3 years working on his own company called Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing, he designed the Induction Motor, one of the most powerful inventions of history.
  • 1889: Tesla patented the Tesla Coil.
  • 1896: Tesla made the 1st X-ray imaging machine.
  • 1898: He made his demonstration of teleautomation. I was the 1st time ever that something were controlled wireless.
  • 1943: On January 7th, he died alone in Room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel.
